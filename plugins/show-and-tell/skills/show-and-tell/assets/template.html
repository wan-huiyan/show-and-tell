🍳 Why the nightly numbers were late

Every night a job cooks tomorrow's reports before 6am — lately it's been running late. Think of it as a kitchen prepping the next day's meals overnight. We timed the kitchen, found where the hours go, and found a fix. Good news, and one honest catch.

running 4h 10m — misses the 6am bell most of it is re-doing yesterday's work a fix could roughly halve it measured on 5 nights · nothing changed yet

★ The one-paragraph version

The kitchen is slow mostly because it re-chops the same onions every single night — about 70% of the time is spent re-doing work whose ingredients didn't change since yesterday. On top of that, a faster oven we already paid for is never actually switched on. If we keep yesterday's chopped onions and flip the oven on, the run should drop to roughly 2 hours — back before the bell. The honest catch: we watched 5 normal nights; one rare "big delivery" night (a data spike) we can't yet predict, and the fix helps less on those.

1 What's going on, in the kitchen

🔪 Today — re-chopping everything

Each night the kitchen starts from scratch: re-chops, re-measures, re-cooks everything — even the parts that are identical to last night. It's thorough, but most of the effort is repeating itself.

🧊 The idea — keep what didn't change

Chop the onions once, keep them in the fridge, and only re-chop what's actually new. Plus turn on the oven we already have. Same meals, far less time.

Engineer's note: "re-chopping" = recomputing partitions whose source rows are unchanged (no incremental/caching layer); "the oven we paid for" = a parallelism setting present in config but not enabled in the prod runner. See flag-gated-feature-dark-in-prod.

2 Where the hours actually go

We timed each stage across 5 nights. Here's the split.

re-doing unchanged work
~70%
genuinely new work

Two-thirds of the night is the kitchen repeating itself — which is exactly the part the "keep the onions" fix removes.

💡The surprise — the fast oven was never on

The recipe says "use the big oven" (it'd cook 3 trays at once), but in the actual kitchen that oven was switched off the whole time. Free speed we'd already bought, sitting unused.

⚖︎ Being straight with you (the limits)

  • We watched 5 normal nights. The "~2h" is an estimate from those — a typical night, not a guarantee.
  • Big-delivery nights are different. When a flood of new data arrives, more of it is genuinely new work, so the "keep the onions" trick helps less. We can't yet predict those nights.
  • Nothing is changed yet — this is a measurement + a plan, fully reversible if we proceed.
